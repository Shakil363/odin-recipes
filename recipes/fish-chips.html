Fish and Chips

A classic British-style fish and chips recipe

Fish and chips is a classic dish featuring crispy, battered white fish and golden fries. The fish is coated in a light, bubbly batter, fried until golden, then served with hot, twice-fried chips and optional tartar sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 white fish fillets (like cod, haddock, or pollock)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water or beer (for extra crispiness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into thick slices or sticks, about 1 cm thick.
  2. Soak the cut potatoes in cold water for about 30 minutes to remove excess starch (optional but helps with crispiness).
  3. Drain and pat the potatoes completely dry with a kitchen towel.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer to around 320°F (160°C).
  5. Fry the potatoes in batches for about 3-4 minutes or until they start to soften and lightly color. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Let the chips cool for a few minutes.
  7. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  8. Gradually add sparkling water (or beer) to the dry ingredients, whisking until the batter is smooth and thick.
  9. Increase oil temperature to 350°F (175°C).
  10. Pat fish fillets dry, then dip each fillet in batter, allowing any excess to drip
  11. Fry for 4-5 minutes, or until the batter is golden brown and the fish is cooked through. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  12. Increase oil temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  13. Fry the chips again for another 2-3 minutes, or until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels and sprinkle with salt.
  14. Serve the hot, crispy fish with the golden chips on the side. Add malt vinegar, tartar sauce, and a lemon wedge for extra flavor.
